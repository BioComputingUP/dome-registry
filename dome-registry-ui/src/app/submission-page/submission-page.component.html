Why Submit Your Methods to the DOME Registry?

Increase Visibility & Discoverability

Make your machine learning methods more visible and discoverable by the research community and industry practitioners.

Promote Reproducibility & Transparency

Commit to transparent science by providing the detailed information others need to understand, replicate, or build upon your work.

Facilitate Method Sharing & Reuse

Enable efficient sharing and adaptation of methods, reducing redundancy and accelerating scientific progress.

Gain Recognition for Method Development

Receive credit for your methodological contributions. Submissions can be linked to your ORCID profile, and recognition provided via APICURON direct to your ORCID (see DOME Registry account page).

Meet Funder & Publisher Requirements

Align with increasing expectations from funders and journals for comprehensive method sharing as part of the publication process. Find out more on our journals page.

Centralise Your Methods

Keep a standardised and accessible record of your lab or institution's machine learning outputs.

What can I submit to the DOME Registry?

The DOME Registry focuses on capturing detailed descriptions of machine learning (ML) methods that have been disseminated through scientific literature. Our goal is to create a structured, searchable repository of these methods to enhance their visibility, understanding, and reproducibility.

You can submit methods described in:
  • Peer-Reviewed Publications: Methods detailed in formally published journal articles or conference papers.
  • Pre-prints: Methods shared via pre-print servers (e.g., arXiv, bioRxiv). While not yet peer-reviewed, making these methods findable and visible early is valuable.
Types of Machine Learning Method Information we Capture:

Submissions should aim to include comprehensive details on machine learning methods in alignment with the DOME Recommendations such as:

Data

Describes the datasets used for developing and testing the model, including details on their source, preprocessing steps, and how they were partitioned for training, validation, and testing.

Optimization

Details the process used to train the model, including the objective function, the specific optimisation algorithm (e.g., Adam, SGD), associated hyperparameters (e.g., learning rate, batch size, epochs), regularization techniques, and the computational environment (software libraries, versions, hardware) used for training.

Model

Describes the machine learning model architecture itself, including the type of model (e.g., logistic regression, convolutional neural network), its specific structure (if applicable, e.g., layers), and any intrinsic model parameters set before training.

Evaluation

Outlines how the trained model's performance was assessed, specifying the metrics used (e.g., accuracy, AUC, F1-score), the evaluation strategy (e.g., performance on a held-out test set, cross-validation), and the results obtained.

Submission Requirements

  • The primary requirement is that the method has been described in a citable publication, pre-print or document with a DOI
  • Your entry should provide sufficient detail, complementing the original publication, to allow another expert to understand the nuances of the method's implementation
  • Submissions are structured using the DOME Wizard, which guides you in providing the necessary information fields

Submission Process

1

Prepare Your Method

Gather all necessary details, steps, materials, parameters, and other relevant information for your method. Refer to our Training page for tips on effective curation.

2

Sign In

Access the submission system by signing in with your DOME Registry account, which uses your ORCID credentials. If you don't have an account, you can create one easily via ORCID. [Link to DOME Registry Account page]

3

Use the DOME Wizard

Launch the "DOME Wizard submit" tool. This user-friendly interface will guide you through structuring and entering your method details field-by-field, ensuring necessary metadata is captured.

4

Submit for Review/Registration

Once complete, submit your entry through the Wizard. Depending on the workflow, there may be automated checks or a brief curation overview before it becomes publicly available in the registry.

5

Method Published

Your method is assigned a persistent identifier and becomes searchable within the DOME Registry.
